Future Problem Solvers

The Program

Future Problem Solvers (FPS) is a set of competitions involving thinking futuristically. Global Issues Problem Solving is a competition in which a team of students use a six step problem solving process to analyze a scenario set in the future. They have to follow a very specific format in their writing. They research the topic in advance and should include information from their research in their booklet.

In addition, students must perform a skit demonstrating their action plan. This skit is required, though the points from the skit don't apply to overall scores.

The 6 Steps

  1. Challenges: identify up to 16 challenges in the future scene
  2. Underlying Problem: determine which challenge you consider the most important
  3. Solutions: invent up to 16 solutions to the underlying problem, using futuristic thinking
  4. Criteria: write five criteria by which your solutions should be analyzed
  5. Criteria (pt 2): Analyze each solution using the criteria, to determine the best solution for your underlying problem
  6. Action Plan: write a persuasive essay about why your winning solution is the best, making sure to flesh out your solution in the process

Other types of competition involved in FPS are Community Problem Solving, Scenario Writing, and Scenario Performance. Community Problem Solving is an activity in which students identify a problem in their community and work on a plan to help with that problem. Scenario Writing is basically futuristic creative writing on a specific, researched topic. Scenario Performance is similar to Scenario Writing, but the story is performed orally.

Georgia Future Problem Solvers hold the State FPS Bowl in Athens, GA in March.

The winners of the state bowl are invited to attend the International Conference, held by Future Problem Solving Program International in June. In 2016, the IC will be held at Michigan State University in East Lansing, MI.
